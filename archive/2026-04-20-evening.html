The Touska Seizure — What It Changes

Prior escalation cycles came from the IRGC (Apr 18 Hormuz reclosure). The Touska seizure is a US action — the Navy detaining a 294m Iranian-flagged container vessel in the Gulf of Oman. Iran called it a ceasefire violation. The IRGC responded with drone attacks on US warships. British military described the situation as “critical.”

This is analytically significant because it reveals a dual-track incoherence on the US side that mirrors Iran’s. State/NSC dispatches Vance/Witkoff/Kushner to Islamabad; the Navy seizes an Iranian vessel the same weekend. Either this was a deliberate pressure tactic (harden the blockade to force IRGC concessions before expiry), or it was an internal disconnect. The same structural problem that makes Iran ungovernable — one actor speaks while another shoots — is now visible in Washington.
