Iran / Middle East Geopolitics Breaking

Delta since last run
Witkoff + Kushner confirmed for Pakistan Saturday. This transforms the Araghchi trip from bilateral theater to potential Round 2. The White House claiming "seen some progress" before talks start is new — they did NOT say this before Round 1. Third carrier arrival may be what forced Iran to "reach out." But the Apr 17 precedent (Araghchi opens, IRGC closes) remains the cautionary template.

What happened today

Morning (pre-market): Third US aircraft carrier (USS George H.W. Bush) arrived in Middle East — three carriers for first time since 2003 Iraq invasion. 12 accompanying ships, 200+ aircraft, 15,000 troops per Al Jazeera. Hegseth: blockade "as long as it takes," "going global". Afternoon (the breakthrough): White House confirmed Witkoff and Kushner will fly to Pakistan Saturday morning for "direct talks" with Iranian counterparts. Karoline Leavitt: "The Iranians reached out and asked for an in-person conversation, as President Trump had asked them to do." WH has "seen some progress." CNBC. Vance NOT attending but on standby — will participate via phone. Haaretz: Araghchi carrying "Tehran's proposal" — Pakistani security sources say counter-proposal in play.

NPR: 20,000 seafarers remain stranded in the Gulf. Humanitarian clock ticking alongside the diplomatic one.

The structural question

The Apr 17 template: Araghchi announced Hormuz open → IRGC reimposed closure within 24h, publicly humiliated him. If this is repeating, Saturday talks are dead on arrival. But two things are different this time:

  1. Iran "reached out" (WH claim). If true, there may be internal authorization that wasn't present Apr 17.
  2. Three carriers. The military pressure channel is maxed. IRGC has to calculate whether continued refusal leads to kinetic escalation, not just more blockade.

The bear case: White House oversells diplomatic momentum. "Seen some progress" could mean "Iran returned a phone call." The +13pp Polymarket move priced in genuine talks; if they collapse Saturday, the unwind is sharp.
