Iran / Geopolitics Format Uncertain

Delta since morning
Iran's FM spokesperson denied any direct meeting with the US. Tasnim (IRGC-linked) echoed the denial. Talks may shift to proximity format (indirect, through Pakistan) and possibly Monday rather than Saturday. The Apr 17 pattern — US announces optimism, Iran walks back — is partially repeating.

The afternoon breakthrough needs qualification. The White House framed the Pakistan trip as "direct talks" with Iranian counterparts. By late afternoon, Iran's FM spokesperson Baghaei stated: "No meeting is planned to take place between Iran and the US." Iran says its "observations" will be conveyed through Pakistan. Tasnim, associated with the IRGC, also disputed the direct talks framing.

Two sources suggest the actual meeting could be Monday, after separate bilateral sessions with Pakistani mediators. This transforms the setup from "Round 2 Saturday" to something closer to proximity talks — Witkoff/Kushner in one room, Araghchi in another, Pakistan shuttling messages.

This is materially less bullish than the afternoon framing but not a collapse. Both sides ARE sending people to Islamabad. The key question remains unchanged: does Araghchi carry IRGC-authorized terms? Tasnim's rejection of direct talks is a cautionary signal — it echoes the IRGC's pattern of constraining Araghchi's diplomatic latitude. But it could also be domestic face-saving while substantive engagement happens through back-channels.
